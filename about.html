About

Introduction:

I am a UX/UI designer with the heart of an artist. I have very distinct mind because I can see the beauty in various disciplines. I can design, code, and draw. I can see the beauty in a MS Excel formula, a script of code, the user experience of a product or service, or piece of art. I am a huge tech history nerd and my favorite books are the Steve Jobs biography and The Innovators by Walter Isaacson.

What introduced you to design?

My first experience UX/UI and product design was when I was young and I got my hands on the very first Apple iPod a friend let me try. Growing up and until this day music is a huge passion for me and I can listen to just about any genre. Iâ€™ve had cd players, mp3, mini-disc you name it. Then I finally got a chance to see what true design was with the very first Apple iPod. The Apple iPod changed the game for me because I could connect with a product and knew that the designers had created something that made my user experience so easy to use and the user interface was so clean and aesthetically pleasing.

Some of my favorite tech companies that inspired me include:

Apple: Apple has changed the game on so many fronts and has been trendsetting in the tech industry for decades.

Microsoft: I would never have had my first computer without Microsoft or my first real job. MS Excel is such a beautiful tool and allowed me to find so many jobs early in my career.

Google: When I first started using the Google search I was blown away at how much better it was compared with all the older search engines. The UX was a game changer for the search industry.

Why is design important to you? How can you use design to make a difference?

Design is important to me because it allows all humans to interact with various objects and services with ease. Design can be found in many forms from fashion, automotive, and apps. Good design allows companies to succeed otherwise users will flock to another product or service. We live in a society that relies on various products and services that must have great design. Otherwise business and governments will fail us - it is imperative that companies hire great designers for the future.
