Bio

I am a UX/UI designer with a passion for design, code and art. I have the unique ability to harness both sides of my brain because I am analytical and artistic.

My career has been as an Analyst working with various companies including small start ups, non-profits, and large corporate organisations. I have worked on projects designing and testing applications and analyzing large sets of data with advanced MS Excel techniques and SQL queries. I have worked with Operations Mangers, Marketing Mangers, Product Managers, UX/UI Designers, and Developers both locally and remotely.

Design has been around for ages and only most recently with the internet revolution a new pardigm has shifted in our society. More and more consumers and business's rely on software in their daily lives. The software or applications that have a lasting impact are the ones that can adapt and change with the flow of time. Design iterates every year. The best example of this is the iPhone, every year Apple designs a new iPhone or else their consumers will gravitate to another new and exciting phone. The same can be said for web and mobile apps. It is paramount that industries hire good Designers that understand the market trends and can create engaging products.

What introduced you to design?

My first experience with UX/UI was when I was young and I got my hands on the very first Apple iPod. I love music and I had all types of devices back in the day including cd players, mp3, and mini-disc. Then I finally got a chance to see what true design was with the very first Apple iPod. The Apple iPod changed the game for me because I not only aprcieted the great hardware design, I also loved the software design. The iPod was so easy and inutitive to use and I always try to create products that will be easy to use for the end-user.
